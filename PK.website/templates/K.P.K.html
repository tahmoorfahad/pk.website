History and Culture

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), formerly known as the North-West Frontier Province, has been a gateway to South Asia for centuries. The province is known for its rich Pashtun culture, historical significance along the Silk Road, and legendary hospitality.

Geography

KPK is located in northwestern Pakistan, bordered by Afghanistan to the west and north, Gilgit-Baltistan to the northeast, Azad Kashmir and Punjab to the east, and Balochistan to the south. The region is known for its mountainous terrain, including parts of the Hindu Kush range.

Economy

The province's economy is diverse, with agriculture, mining, and tourism playing significant roles. KPK is rich in natural resources and has significant hydroelectric power generation capacity. The region is also known for its handicrafts and traditional industries.
